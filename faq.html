Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most — about reporting pets, volunteering, and how it all works.

General

Paws & Found is a community-driven platform that helps reunite lost pets with their families. Anyone can report a lost or found pet, browse current listings, or volunteer to help search, foster, or spread the word.

Yes — completely. Reporting a pet, browsing listings, and volunteering are all free. We're a community effort, not a paid service.

No account needed. Just fill out the report form whenever you need to — no sign-up required.

Reporting a Lost or Found Pet

Head to the Report a Pet page, choose whether you lost or found a pet, and fill in as much detail as you can. A clear photo, the location, and a good description make the biggest difference in getting a fast match.

Your report stays listed until the pet is reunited with their family. Once that happens, please update or remove your listing so volunteers can focus their attention on pets who are still missing.

Email us at help@pawsandfound.org with the details, and we'll help you update or take down the listing.

Check the Found Pets page regularly, share your listing with neighbors and local community groups, post physical flyers nearby, and call local shelters and vet clinics directly — many lost pets are found within the first 48 hours.

Found a Pet

Approach slowly, check for a collar or tag, and get them scanned for a microchip — most vet clinics and shelters do this for free with no appointment needed. Then report them on our Report a Pet page so we can help track down their family. See our full step-by-step guide for more detail.

Reach out to local shelters and rescue groups for guidance on next steps, and keep your report active here in case the owner is still out there searching.

Volunteering

Visit the How to Help page and fill out the volunteer sign-up form. Tell us how you'd like to help — fostering, flyers, transport, and more — and we'll reach out about opportunities near you.

Not for most roles. Sharing alerts, distributing flyers, transport, or reporting sightings don't require any animal experience — just willingness to help.

Privacy

Yes, by design — your contact details appear on your report so anyone who recognizes your pet can reach you directly, without delay. We'd recommend sharing only what's needed to be contacted (e.g. an email or one phone number).
Still have questions?

Email us at help@pawsandfound.org and we'll get back to you as soon as we can.
